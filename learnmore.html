FishCRRM 1.1

Comprehensive overview of the Fisheries and Coastal Resilient Resource Management (FishCRRM) Sub-component 1.1

Project Overview

FishCRRM (Fisheries and Coastal Resilient Resource Planning and Management) 1.1 is a critical sub-component of the Philippine Fisheries and Coastal Resiliency (FishCoRe) Project that focuses on strengthening fisheries management in the Philippines through an Ecosystem Approach to Fisheries Management (EAFM).

Sub-component 1.1 will support FMA institutional setup, FP development, and the formulation and implementation of at least one FMP for priority fish stocks and at least one MHMRP in each FMA through technical studies, provision of equipment, and stakeholder consultations. To support project M&E, the Project will review existing surveys and data-collection systems and support the design and establishment of a modern information management system. Implementation of the FMPs/CMHPRMPs will be reviewed annually, and a formal evaluation against their objectives and indicators will be conducted every three years to facilitate adaptive management. It will also support climate-smart RDE services and the development and implementation of IEC strategies to ensure stakeholder buy-in for the FMA management objectives and approaches.

Key Interventions/Activities

Enhanced Governance

Development of FMA Framework, FMPs and related monitoring, control and surveillance (MCS) strategy as well as marine habitat management and restoration plans, including related analytical work.

Capacity Development

Institutional set-up and capacity building for FMA management.

Strengthened Monitoring

Establishment of a monitoring and evaluation (M&E) system to support adaptive FMA management.

Implementation Structure

The FishCRRM 1.1 Component operates through a multi-level governance structure:

  • National Level: Provides overall policy direction, coordination, and technical support for the implementation of the component.
  • FMA Level: Focuses on the management and coordination of activities within specific Fishery Management Areas (FMA 6 and FMA 9).
  • Regional Level: Ensures effective implementation at the regional level, coordinating with local government units and stakeholders.

Coverage Areas

The FishCRRM 1.1 Sub-component will be implemented in two out of 12 FMAs in the country. These are in the coastal and municipal waters of FMA 6 and FMA 9, which were selected through a two-stage, multi-stakeholder selection process adopting the following criteria: (i) level of vulnerability to natural and man-made threats, (ii) status of fisheries resources, (iii) access to sustainable- and resilient-investment opportunities, (iv) contribution of fisheries production to the national economy; and (v) institutional capacity.
